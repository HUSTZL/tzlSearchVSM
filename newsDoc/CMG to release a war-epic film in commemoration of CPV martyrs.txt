CMG to release a war-epic film in commemoration of CPV martyrs
China Media Group (CMG) on Monday published a series of film posters and a trailer for the movie "Crossing the Yalu River" to commemorate the 71st anniversary of the War to Resist U.S. Aggression and Aid Korea. The film pays tribute to the Chinese People's Volunteers (CPV) army who fought in the war and shows their bravery, patriotism and devotion fighting against their enemy.

The film presents fierce battles, skirmishes, defensive war and tunnel warfare and showcases how CPV martyrs defended peace, justice and the country with their lives.

Based on actual events, the film is the culmination of 10 months of work from a star-studded cast and crew. The release "Crossing the Yalu River" will enable more new viewers to have a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of the war.

The movie is co-produced by the CMG Film, Drama and Documentary Programming Center, the China Television Production Center and Huaxia Film Distribution.
