NBA-leading Warriors rally to beat Cavaliers 104-89

Stephen Curry of Golden State Warriors celebrates during the NBA match against Cleveland Cavaliers in Cleveland, U.S., November 18, 2021. /CFP

The Golden State Warriors came back from 13 points down to beat the Cleveland Cavaliers 104-89 in NBA, with Stephen Curry contributing 40 points, 20 of which from the final quarter on Thursday.

Nemanja Bjelica added 14 points for the Warriors. Andrew Wiggins had 12 points, and Draymond Green had four points and 14 rebounds. The Warriors have now extended their unbeaten run against the Cavaliers to nine games.

Elsewhere on Thursday, the Utah Jazz beat the Toronto Raptors 119-103, as Rudy Gay scored 20 points, including five 3-pointers in his season debut.

Though given only 18 minutes of playing time, Gay also got five rebounds besides being efficient earning scores. He missed the previous 14 games of this season, recovering from surgery on his right heel.

Despite being overshadowed by the growing injury list, the Philadelphia 76ers beat the Denver Nuggets 103-89 to put an end to their losing streak. Tyrese Maxey scored 22 points, and Seth Curry added 20 for the 76ers.
