Schools shut indefinitely in Indian capital Delhi as smog worsens
Schools have been shut down indefinitely with the air pollution reaching dangerous levels in Indian capital Delhi. Some trucks have been barred from entering the city. Several coal-fired power plants have been shut down. The city is one of the world's most polluted. Last week, pollution levels hit over 30 times the maximum limit recommended by the World Health Organization. Street vendors in Delhi have found themselves in the thick of the city's air pollution.
